Introduction

Design thinking is an iterative process in which you seek to understand your users, challenge assumptions, redefine problems and create innovative solutions which you can prototype and test. The overall goal is to identify alternative strategies and solutions that are not instantly apparent with your initial level of understanding.

Design thinking is more than just a process; it opens up an entirely new way to think, and it offers a collection of hands-on methods to help you apply this new mindset.

Principles of Design Thinking:

  • The Human Rule
    All Design is social in nature
  • The Ambiguity Rule
    Ambiguity is inevitable - experiment at the limits of your knowledge
  • All Design is Redesign
    Technology and Social Circumstances may change but Basic Human Needs remain unchanged
  • The Tangibility Rule
    Prototypes help to make ideas tangible, enabling designers to communicate them effectively
Empathize

Identify and Understand your problem:

  • Drop Assumptions, Judgements, and Biases.
  • Observe. View users and their behavior in the context of their lives.
  • Engage. Interact with and interview users through both scheduled and short ‘intercept’ encounters.
  • Immerse. Experience what your user experiences.

Goals:

  • Scope your target market
  • Understand your challenge
  • Create an Empathy Map
  • Does everyone in the team share the same goal?

Ideate

Exploring wide solution space
Data from Empathize to:

  • Step beyond your comfort zone and be imaginative
  • Harness diverse perspective and strength of your team.
  • Generate ideas by exploring unexpected areas.
Ideation is about brainstorming ideas
  • The goal is to generate as many ideas as possible
  • At this point your team startsto imagine possibilities
  • Do not check for feasibility and viability at the beginning of ideation, this is done later during prioritization

Impact and Effort Matrix

The impact effort matrix is a visual tool designed to help teams prioritize tasks and maximize their efficiency while ensuring that their goals are aligned. Set up as a four-quadrant grid, the matrix organizes tasks into four categories: Quick wins: Projects with maximum impact that require minimal effort.

IDEATION ACTIVITY:

  1. Create a Cartesian Plane
  2. Based on HMWs, brainstorm ideas.
  3. Cluster your ideas.
  4. Prioritize your ideas for the upcoming prototyping phase.
  5. Identify difficulty and impact of ideas.
GOAL: generate as many ideas as possible, cluster & vote

Prototype
Getting ideas out of your head and into the physical world.

GOAL:

  • Learn
    A picture is worth a thousand words; a prototype is worth a thousand pictures.
  • Explore
    Build to think and spark additional ideas.
  • Fail quickly and cheaply
    Test ideas without investing too much time and money.

PROTOTYPE ACTIVITY:

  • Prototype your solution based on the ideas you prioritized.
  • Remember, the evaluation criteria are creativity and simplicity of your solution, how well it fits your users’ needs, as well as your presentation.
  • Think about how you want to pitch your solution / who will take what role.
GOAL: Visualize your idea, make it tangible

Test
The chance to refine solutions and make them better

Validate your Ideas

  • Informs the next iteration of prototypes
  • Test with your users and in their context; it’s another chance to build empathy
  • Refine your POV – sometimes testing reveals that the problem frame needs to change.
GET FEEDBACK
  • Create a storyline
  • Capture and later synthesize all feedback
  • Ideate how the feedback can be worked into the next iteration of your solution
  • Take your idea, interview your USERS and show them your prototype.
  • Listen carefully to what they say.

VALIDATION ACTIVITY:

  1. Validate your idea
  2. Get feedback
GOAL: Get feedback for you idea & iterate.
